C. Game brokering for gaming communities (gaming community)

1. Declarations of intent by the player

1.1. Faber informs the player of the amount of the fees that Faber will pay to the respective state lottery company for concluding the gaming contract. It is then Faber's task to conclude a partnership agreement with other players on behalf of the player to form a gaming community. The gaming community should have a certain number of members (e.g. 40 or 100) and - with Faber's mediation - submit lottery tips for state-approved lotteries to a lottery organizer.

1.2. The player authorizes Faber to carry out all legal transactions to establish the gaming community and to accept declarations of intent from the gaming community. He also authorizes Faber to negotiate gaming contracts between the gaming community and the respective lottery organizer on behalf of the gaming community in accordance with the product description (see section 3).

1.3. Faber is entitled to participate in gaming communities itself if not all of the gaming community shares can be awarded by players by the closing date (see section 4). In this case, the same rules apply to Faber as to the other players.

1.4. By purchasing a share in the gaming community, the player also instructs and authorizes Faber to inform him by email if the gaming community wins and to instruct a trustee on behalf of the gaming community to claim any winnings from the respective lottery organizer, to collect them and, after the gaming community is dissolved, to distribute them equally to the members of the gaming community (see section B.8 for more details).

2. Declaration of acceptance by Faber

2.1. Faber accepts the offer to form a gaming community in accordance with section C.1. by sending an email to the registered email address. In some cases, a confirmation of receipt is sent before acceptance so that the player knows that his order has been received. The offer is only accepted by the declaration of acceptance (which also includes the game receipt for the gaming contract with the gaming community).

2.2. Even if the player is registered with Faber, he is not entitled to a declaration of acceptance. Acceptance will not be declared if the player is blocked.

3. Game contract, game receipt

3.1. Faber concludes a game contract with a lottery organizer on the basis of the offer as a representative of the gaming community. Faber is entitled to determine the individual betting lines of the gaming community at its own discretion. If you participate in a gaming community repeatedly, you are not entitled to retain the game lines; the game lines are usually different for each draw and are communicated to the player accordingly. When concluding the game contract, Faber will also conclude the general terms and conditions of the lottery organizer specifically selected in the individual case (see section A.1.). If you participate in several lotteries at the same time, the relevant general terms and conditions of the respective lottery organizers apply to the game contracts concluded in each case. The special provisions of the respective lottery organizers apply to special draws (see also section B.4.2.5).

3.2. Faber is the receiving agent of the gaming community with regard to any declarations of acceptance or contract confirmations from the state lottery company.

3.3. Faber sends the player and the other members of the gaming community a game receipt by email after the conclusion of the gaming contract ("game receipt"). The game receipt contains the player's personal details, details of the specific game brokerage order, the numbers selected or the lottery number, the name of the lotteries and the number of the game ticket.
